Bunny Chow also known as Kota Mince

Bunny Chow is a South African staple. It originated in the Durban Indian community. It basically is half a loaf of bread, hollowed out and filled with either mince, mutton, lamb, chicken, beef and vegetables

Ingredients

  • 1kg Lean beef mince
  • Oil to base pot
  • 1tsp Funnel Seeds
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • 4 Cloves
  • A sprig of curry leaves
  • 1tsp crushed green chillies
  • 1Large onion finely crushed
  • 1/2 tsp tumeric
  • 2tbles crushed ginger and garlic mix
  • 2tsp sugar
  • 3 level tble medium chillie powder
  • 2tsp coriander/cummin powder
  • 2 tomatoes blended
  • 1tsp garam masala
  • Chopped fresh coriander
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

  1. Heat oil in pot
  2. Saute onion in heated oil til it becomes golden brown
  3. Add cloves, bay leaves and funnel in pot
  4. Stir
  5. Add curry leaves, chillies, ginger and garlic mix and tumeric
  6. Braise for 3-4 minutes
  7. Add spices, garam masala and sugar to pot and mix well
  8. Add meat and salt
  9. Mix until mince is covered in masala
  10. Cover pot and braise on moderate heat for a few minutes
  11. Stir from time to time to ensure mince is properly braised
  12. Add water as needed to create saucy mince
  13. Add fresh coriander once mince is fully mixed
  14. Once done, fill hollowed loaf with mince
Enjoy!
